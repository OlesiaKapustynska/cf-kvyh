About Vasyl-Yakiv Kapustynskyi

Vasyl-Yakiv Kapustynskyi was born on January 1, 1998, in the village of Bilyi Potik, Ternopil Oblast, Ukraine. From an early age, Vasyl-Yakiv exhibited a profound dedication to his community and nation, which guided his life's path. Educated at Ridoduby Secondary School, he furthered his studies at Chortkiv College of Economics and Entrepreneurship, where he chaired the student council and participated in numerous theatrical performances. He pursued higher education in socio-humanitarian studies at the Western Ukrainian National University, earning a Bachelor's degree in "Social and Cultural Activity Management" and a Master's degree in "Social Work and Social Policy."

Professional and Activist Life

Vasyl-Yakiv served as a corporate social responsibility manager at Continental Farmers Group from 2019 to 2022, where he was instrumental in implementing social, sports, and youth projects across Western Ukraine. Notably, he coordinated the "Smart Garden" project, teaching children about cultivating vegetables and medicinal herbs. He also promoted small household cooperatives for medicinal herb cultivation, conducted thematic seminars, and organized educational trips. Vasyl-Yakiv tirelessly worked to create jobs in rural areas, fostering the development of a self-employed middle class in Ukraine. He planned to establish a facility for drying and packaging medicinal herbs and berries and participated in international grant projects led by the Ternopil Agrarian Advisory Service and CO SOK "Faini Hospodari."

Contributions to Arts and Culture

In addition to his community and professional endeavors, Vasyl-Yakiv was a talented actor. He featured in the film "Maria" (2021) and the educational series "Knowledge During War." As a member of the theatrical team at the Western Ukrainian National University, he played lead roles in productions such as "Matchmaking on Honcharivka," "Kaidasheva Family," and "Night on the Mountain."

Military Service and Honors

Vasyl-Yakiv's patriotic spirit led him to serve in the Armed Forces of Ukraine during the Russia-Ukraine war. Tragically, he lost his life on February 2, 2023, in Kreminna, Luhansk Oblast. In recognition of his bravery and dedication, he was posthumously awarded the Order of Courage III Class and honored as a Citizen of Ternopil in 2023.

Goals of the Foundation

Supporting Youth and Community Projects: Initiating and funding programs that empower young people and communities, following the example set by Vasyl-Yakiv’s work with Continental Farmers Group and other organizations. Promoting Arts and Culture: Encouraging participation in the arts through scholarships, grants, and support for theater and film projects. Developing Rural Areas: Advancing agricultural projects and cooperative initiatives to foster economic growth and self-sufficiency in rural Ukraine. Honoring Military Service: Providing support and recognition to servicemen and their families, ensuring that the sacrifices of heroes like Vasyl-Yakiv are never forgotten.
